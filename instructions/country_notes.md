# Country Notes

Use these notes when comparing undergraduate major systems across China, South Korea, Japan, the United Kingdom, the United States, and Australia.

## China

- Common terms: 本科专业、专业类、学科门类、学院、系、培养方案、教学计划。
- A university college or school is not always the same as a major.
- Official undergraduate major names may connect to the Ministry of Education major catalog.
- Distinguish 专业名称、专业类、授予学位门类、培养方案、就业方向.

## South Korea

- Common terms: 학과, 전공, 학부, 대학, 교육과정.
- 학부 can contain multiple majors or tracks.
- Some universities admit students by broad division and allocate majors later.
- Distinguish department, major, division, college/faculty, curriculum.

## Japan

- Common terms: 学部, 学科, 専攻, コース, カリキュラム.
- 学部 is an undergraduate faculty or school, not always a major.
- 学科 is often closer to a program or department.
- Distinguish faculty, department, specialization, course/track.

## United Kingdom

- Common terms: course, subject, programme, degree, module, honours, placement year, sandwich year.
- In the UK, course often means a full undergraduate degree program, not one class.
- Major-style flexibility is usually lower than in the United States.
- Distinguish degree title, course, module, department/school, professional accreditation.

## United States

- Common terms: major, program, department, college/school, concentration, minor, general education, liberal arts.
- US undergraduate study often includes general education, major/minor, electives, and possible major changes.
- Major may not be fixed from the first day at every institution.
- Distinguish major, degree type, concentration, department, general education.

## Australia

- Common terms: course, program, major, bachelor degree, unit, handbook, honours, accreditation, placement.
- Course/program can mean a full degree, while major can be a specialization within a degree.
- Official handbooks are often key sources.
- Distinguish bachelor degree, course/program, major, unit, accreditation.

## Translation Rules

- Do not mechanically translate every term as "专业".
- Preserve local terms in parentheses when the distinction matters.
- Explain naming ambiguity when comparing countries.
- Keep official program titles in their original language when needed, then explain in Chinese.
- Use `data/major_aliases.yaml` for search expansion when the user provides a broad or translated major name.
- Treat aliases as search aids, not as proof that two programmes are equivalent.
